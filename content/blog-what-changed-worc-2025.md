# What Changed: WORC's November 2025 Rules Explained for Employers

*Published March 2026* | *Reading time: 8 minutes*

On October 22, 2025, the Workforce Opportunities and Residency Cayman (WORC) announced a sweeping set of changes to work permit application requirements. These changes took effect on **November 1, 2025**, and were subsequently codified through the **[Immigration (Transition) Amendment Act, 2025](https://legislation.ky/laws/immigration-transition-amendment-2025)** passed in December 2025.

If you're an employer in the Cayman Islands — whether you're a small business filing one work permit a year or a large firm filing dozens — these changes affect you directly. Here's exactly what changed, why it changed, and what you need to do about it.

---

## The Big Picture: Why These Changes Happened

The Cayman Islands government has a longstanding policy of **Caymanian preference** in employment, enshrined in the [Immigration (Transition) Act](https://legislation.ky/laws/immigration-transition-act). The principle is straightforward: if a qualified Caymanian is available for a role, they should be hired before a work permit is issued to a foreign national.

In practice, the previous system had enforcement gaps. Employers could technically comply with advertising requirements while doing little to genuinely consider Caymanian candidates. Postings could be vague. Feedback was optional. Documentation was minimal.

The November 2025 changes close those gaps. The government's message is clear: employers must demonstrate **genuine, documented effort** to hire Caymanians — not just check a box.

The financial impact is significant. Immigration fee changes are projected to generate **CI$15.4 million in additional government revenue** in 2026 alone, providing both funding for enforcement and a financial incentive for employers to get compliance right the first time.

## Change-by-Change Breakdown: Before vs. After

### 1. Advertising Requirements

| | Before Nov 2025 | After Nov 2025 |
|---|---|---|
| **Which positions** | Full-time positions requiring work permits | **ALL positions**, including part-time, temporary, and contract |
| **Where** | WORC Portal (plus other channels) | WORC Portal (mandatory; other channels supplementary) |
| **Duration** | Minimum advertising period | **14+ consecutive calendar days** on WORC Portal |
| **Content** | Basic job information | **9 mandatory fields** including exact salary, all benefits, hours, location |

**What this means:** You can no longer post a vague listing for two weeks and call it compliance. Every posting needs complete, specific information — and the 14-day clock only counts if the posting is fully compliant.

### 2. Candidate Tracking

| | Before Nov 2025 | After Nov 2025 |
|---|---|---|
| **Who to track** | Candidates who progressed to interview | **Every Caymanian who applied** |
| **What to record** | General hiring outcome | Specific outcome for each applicant (hired, interviewed, screened out, withdrew) |
| **Where to log** | Internal records | **WORC Portal** |

**What this means:** If 50 Caymanians apply and you interview 3, you need documented outcomes for all 50. Immigration isn't just looking at who you hired — they're looking at how you treated every Caymanian applicant.

### 3. Structured Feedback (Entirely New)

| | Before Nov 2025 | After Nov 2025 |
|---|---|---|
| **Required?** | No | **Yes — for every non-hired Caymanian** |
| **Format** | N/A | Structured, specific, tied to job requirements |
| **Detail level** | N/A | Must reference specific qualifications from the posting |
| **Generic acceptable?** | N/A | **No** — "not a fit" or "other candidates stronger" will be rejected |

**What this means:** This is the single biggest operational change. Your hiring managers need to write specific, defensible explanations for why each Caymanian wasn't selected. This requires training, templates, and a process — not something you can improvise at permit application time.

### 4. Documentation Requirements

| | Before Nov 2025 | After Nov 2025 |
|---|---|---|
| **Proof of advertising** | Often self-reported | **Verifiable against WORC Portal records** |
| **Applicant records** | Optional supporting docs | **Mandatory — full list with outcomes** |
| **Feedback** | Not required | **Mandatory structured feedback for all non-hired Caymanians** |
| **Interview evidence** | Rarely requested | **Dates, interviewers, notes expected** |
| **Comparative assessment** | Sometimes requested | **Standard requirement** |

**What this means:** Work permit applications are now documentation-heavy. Think of it as building a legal case: you need to prove, with evidence, that you gave Caymanians genuine consideration and had defensible reasons for hiring a foreign national.

### 5. Fee Structure

| | Before Nov 2025 | After Nov 2025 |
|---|---|---|
| **Application admin fee** | CI$100 | **CI$500** (5x increase) |
| **Annual permit fees** | Previous rate schedule | Restructured — up to **CI$32,400** for senior legal positions |
| **Fee refund on denial** | No refund | **No refund** |

**What this means:** A denied permit now costs you CI$500 in fees alone — before you factor in the cost of re-advertising, re-recruiting, and project delays. Getting it right the first time has never been more financially important.

## The Practical Impact on HR Teams

### You Need a Process, Not Just a Policy

Before November 2025, many employers could handle compliance informally. Post the job, wait the required period, file the permit. That approach no longer works.

You now need:

1. **A compliant posting template** that includes all 9 required fields
2. **An applicant tracking system** that logs every Caymanian applicant with timestamps
3. **A structured feedback framework** that hiring managers can use consistently
4. **A documentation assembly process** for compiling the permit application package
5. **Training for hiring managers** on writing defensible, specific feedback

### Timeline Planning Has Changed

Under the old rules, you could move relatively quickly from decision-to-hire to work permit submission. Now, the minimum timeline looks like this:

- **Day 1:** Post compliant job listing on WORC Portal
- **Days 1–14:** Mandatory advertising period (minimum)
- **Days 14–28:** Evaluate candidates, conduct interviews, document outcomes
- **Days 28–35:** Compile documentation, write structured feedback, prepare application
- **Day 35+:** Submit work permit application

That's **5+ weeks minimum** from the decision to hire internationally to actually submitting the permit application. Plan accordingly.

### The Structured Feedback Challenge

This is where most employers will struggle. Writing structured feedback requires:

- **Specificity:** "The applicant has 3 years of experience; the role requires 7 years in regulatory compliance" — not "insufficient experience"
- **Consistency:** Feedback must align with the requirements listed in the job posting
- **Contemporaneity:** Written during the hiring process, not assembled retroactively
- **Defensibility:** Could withstand scrutiny from an Immigration officer or legal challenge

If your hiring managers currently make decisions based on interviews and gut feel, you need to build a more structured evaluation process.

2. **Check advertising timelines.** Are your postings running for 14+ consecutive days?
