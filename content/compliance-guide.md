# The Complete Employer Guide to WORC Compliance (2026)

**Last updated: March 2026** | Reading time: 12 minutes

If you employ anyone in the Cayman Islands on a work permit — or plan to — the rules changed significantly on November 1, 2025. The [Immigration (Transition) Amendment Act, 2025](https://legislation.ky/laws/immigration-transition-amendment-2025) introduced sweeping new requirements for how employers advertise positions, evaluate Caymanian candidates, and document every step of the hiring process.

This guide breaks down every requirement in plain English, explains what it means for your HR team, and shows you exactly how to stay compliant — whether you handle it manually or use tools like careers.ky to automate the process.

---

## Why This Matters Now

The Cayman Islands government has made compliance enforcement a top priority. The changes aren't cosmetic — they represent a fundamental shift in how Immigration evaluates work permit applications. Employers who don't adapt risk:

- **Denied work permits** — with no refund of the CI$500 application fee
- **Delayed hiring timelines** — adding weeks or months to fill critical roles
- **Legal liability** — potential penalties under the [Immigration (Transition) Act](https://legislation.ky/laws/immigration-transition-act)
- **Reputational damage** — being flagged as a non-compliant employer

The new framework generated an estimated **CI$15.4 million in additional government revenue** in its first year. Immigration has the resources and mandate to enforce it.

## The 6 Requirements Every Employer Must Meet

### Requirement 1: Advertise ALL Positions on the WORC Portal for 14+ Consecutive Days

**What changed:** Previously, some positions — particularly part-time roles — had looser advertising requirements. As of November 1, 2025, **every position** for which you intend to seek a work permit must be advertised on the WORC Job Portal for a minimum of **14 consecutive calendar days**.

**What this means for you:**
- The 14-day clock starts when the posting goes live on the WORC Portal — not on your company website, not on LinkedIn, not in the newspaper
- If a posting is taken down and reposted, the clock resets
- Part-time, temporary, and contract roles are all included
- You cannot submit a work permit application until the 14-day period has elapsed

**Common mistake:** Posting on WORC but with incomplete information (see Requirement 2), which can invalidate the advertising period entirely.

### Requirement 2: Include Complete Job Information in Every Posting

**What changed:** Job postings must now include **all** of the following:

| Required Field | What Immigration Expects |
|---|---|
| **Job title** | Accurate, industry-standard title (not inflated or obscured) |
| **Duties** | Specific, detailed description of day-to-day responsibilities |
| **Qualifications** | Required education, certifications, experience, and skills |
| **Salary** | Exact salary or salary range — not "competitive" or "negotiable" |
| **Benefits** | All benefits including health insurance, pension, housing, travel |
| **Working hours** | Full-time/part-time, expected weekly hours, shift patterns |
| **Job type** | Permanent, temporary, contract, seasonal |
| **Location** | Specific work location in the Cayman Islands |
| **Special conditions** | Any travel requirements, physical demands, or unusual conditions |

**What this means for you:**
- Vague postings will be rejected or flagged
- "Salary: DOE" or "Competitive salary" is no longer acceptable
- Benefits must be itemized, not just "standard benefits package"
- Every field must be completed — there are no optional fields

**Why it matters:** Immigration uses this information to evaluate whether Caymanian applicants were given a fair opportunity. If the posting is vague, they can't verify that qualified Caymanians weren't unfairly screened out.

### Requirement 3: Log the Outcome of Every Caymanian Applicant

**What changed:** This is entirely new. Employers must now record the outcome of **every Caymanian who applies** for a position — not just the candidates who made it to interview stage.

**What this means for you:**
- If 30 Caymanians apply and you interview 5, you need documented outcomes for all 30
- "Outcome" means: hired, interviewed and not selected, screened out at application stage, withdrew, etc.
- The outcome must be logged on the WORC Portal
- This applies even if the Caymanian applicant was clearly unqualified

**Why it matters:** Immigration wants to see that every Caymanian applicant received genuine consideration. Blanket rejections or missing records raise red flags.

### Requirement 4: Provide Structured Feedback for Non-Hired Caymanians

**What changed:** If a Caymanian applicant is **not hired**, you must now provide **structured feedback** explaining why. This is the most significant new requirement and the one most likely to cause permit denials.

**What "structured feedback" means:**
- Specific reasons tied to the job requirements listed in the posting
- Reference to which qualifications, skills, or experience the applicant lacked
- Cannot be generic ("not a good fit," "other candidates were stronger")
- Must be documented and submitted as part of the work permit application

**Example of compliant feedback:**
> "Applicant did not meet the required qualification of a CPA designation as listed in the job posting. Applicant holds a Bachelor's in Accounting but has not completed CPA certification. The role requires signing off on audited financial statements, which requires CPA designation under Cayman Islands regulations."

**Example of non-compliant feedback:**
> "Did not meet requirements." ❌
> "Other candidates were more qualified." ❌
> "Not a cultural fit." ❌

**What this means for you:**
- HR teams need a systematic process for evaluating and documenting candidate assessments
- Hiring managers must provide specific, defensible reasons — not gut feelings
- Every rejection needs a paper trail that links back to the job posting requirements
- This feedback is reviewed by Immigration officers when processing work permit applications

### Requirement 5: Submit Mandatory Supporting Documentation

**What changed:** Work permit applications now require comprehensive supporting documentation proving compliance with Requirements 1–4. This includes:

- Proof of WORC Portal advertising (with dates showing 14+ consecutive days)
- Complete job posting as it appeared on the portal
- List of all Caymanian applicants received
- Outcome documentation for each Caymanian applicant
- Structured feedback for each non-hired Caymanian
- Evidence of interviews conducted (dates, interviewers, notes)
- Comparative assessment showing why the work permit candidate was selected over Caymanian applicants

**What this means for you:**
- You need to maintain organized records from the moment a position is posted
- Documentation must be contemporaneous — created during the hiring process, not retroactively assembled
- Missing documentation is grounds for denial
- Immigration officers will cross-reference your documentation against WORC Portal records

### Requirement 6: Updated Fee Structure

**What changed:** The work permit administration fee increased **5x**, from CI$100 to CI$500 per application. Annual permit fees have also been restructured, with rates up to **CI$32,400** for senior legal positions.

**Selected annual permit fee examples:**

| Role Category | Annual Fee |
|---|---|
| General worker | CI$5,000–CI$12,000 |
| Professional/technical | CI$12,000–CI$20,000 |
| Senior management | CI$20,000–CI$28,000 |
| Senior legal positions | Up to CI$32,400 |

**What this means for you:**
- A denied application costs you CI$500 in non-refundable fees — plus the time and cost of re-advertising for another 14 days
- The financial stakes of non-compliance are significantly higher
- Budget planning for international hires must account for increased permit costs
- Cost-benefit analysis favors getting it right the first time

## The Compliance Timeline: From Job Posting to Work Permit

Here's the complete process under the new rules:

**Week 1: Prepare and Post**
1. Draft job posting with ALL required fields completed
2. Post on WORC Portal
3. 14-day advertising period begins

**Weeks 2–3: Advertising Period**
4. Monitor applications from Caymanian candidates
5. Begin reviewing and documenting outcomes for each applicant
6. Conduct interviews with qualified Caymanian applicants

**Week 3–4: Evaluation and Documentation**
7. Complete structured feedback for each non-hired Caymanian applicant
8. Prepare comparative assessment
9. Compile all supporting documentation

**Week 4+: Application**
10. Submit work permit application with complete documentation package
11. Pay CI$500 application fee
12. Await Immigration decision

**Realistic timeline:** Even in the best case, expect **4–6 weeks** from job posting to work permit submission. If documentation is incomplete, add another cycle.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do these rules apply to permit renewals?
Yes. Renewal applications must also demonstrate ongoing compliance with Caymanian preference requirements, including evidence of advertising and consideration of Caymanian candidates.

### What if no Caymanians apply for the position?
You must still complete the full 14-day advertising period with a compliant posting. Document that no Caymanian applications were received. This is actually the simplest compliance scenario — but you still need the documentation.

### Can I use a recruitment agency to handle compliance?
Yes, but the employer remains ultimately responsible. If your agency cuts corners, it's your work permit that gets denied. Using a platform like careers.ky alongside an agency gives you independent documentation.

### How far back does Immigration look at compliance history?
Immigration can review your compliance history across all previous work permit applications. A pattern of non-compliance can affect future applications.

### What counts as "structured feedback"?
Feedback must reference specific requirements from the job posting and explain why the candidate did not meet them. Generic statements are not acceptable. Each piece of feedback should be 2–5 sentences minimum, directly tied to the qualifications listed in your job posting.

### Is there a grace period for the new requirements?
No. The rules took effect November 1, 2025. All work permit applications submitted after that date must comply with the new requirements.
